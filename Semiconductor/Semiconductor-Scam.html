Semiconductor Scam: The vanished of HSMC's promised to built China’s first 7 nm chips

How did the promise of Hongxing Semiconductor Manufacturing Company come to an end?


By Cao Jingyi, Li Shiwen, Mereen Santirad, Wang Ziwei

Previous Next

HSMC, the government backed manufacturing project has gone belly-up, with a key operator  mired in debt. The project amounted to nearly RMB 128 billion investment according to the local government. The local authority reported that the project was stagnant due to poor planning and shortage of funding. 

 In 2019, Wuhan, Hongxin Semiconductor Factory's construction has stalled;  Source:aijiwei.com

 According to the satellite image in December 2019, tthe construction of the HSMC plant has not yet been completed

Our investigation has found some important evidence of the reality. 

China semiconductor has a history of fraudulent players. Two decades ago, the much-hyped Hanxin, also known as ‘heart of the Han” processor was later discovered to be a scam in which workers were standing off the Motorola brand and replacing it with the Hanxin logo.

Shanghai in 2003, Hanxin One: A 1.1 billion yuan scam completed by migrant workers from the decoration company and doctors from returnees; Source: Google Image

Dekema, a local government backed Nanjing-based chipmaker, announced it was bankrupt because of “financial difficulties” in raising additional funds from investors in July. 

HSMC semiconductor project that planned to produce China’s first 7 nanometer chips was stalled in December, 2019. The contractor, L&K Engineering, a listed company that helped build dust-free rooms and mechanical systems for the production facility, halted its construction because of the delays in payment from HSMC, according to the contractor's half-year earnings report.  

On May 9, 2019, L&K Engineering received the "Notice of Winning Bid" from the HSMC.

In L&K Engineering's 2020 third quarter report, it was pointed out that HSMC had not paid a total of 700 million yuan in contract payments

An employee who was about to join the company decided to quit because of the delay. An online post from the employee read [translated to English] “I resigned from this company at the end of this month. I was originally scheduled to join Hongxing on July 15, but I was notified about the delay at the beginning of the month. I don’t know when I will join.” 

On June 17, 2020, a netizen named Elmer.liu reported on the Chinese question-and-answer website "Zhihu" that HSMC was continuously delaying his entry date; Source: Zhihu

We tried to contact people who knew about  the incident, but refused to be interviewed for reasons for personal safety.

We posted on Zhihu about the issue of the Wuhan government's takeover of HSMC. Some netizens described the ulterior secrets in very secret words.

The comments state that there was a thunderstorm in HSMC. Although we saw a few fish on the beach, and even seemed to see a few sharks under the water. But another wave came, the local state-owned assets took over, and the fish and sharks were hidden again in the water.

On September 12, 2019, HSMC was sued by another contractor, Wuhan Huanyu Engineering Company as a result of the unpaid amount of RMB 41 million for the construction service on November 1, 2019, as reported in China Judgement online website, court case No: GongDXH(2018) No.039.

On September 12, 2019, HSMC and its partner contractor Wuhan Torch Construction Group were convicted of freezing bank accounts and sealing up factory land; 
Source: wenshu.court.gov.cn

After the trial, the court froze the construction and prohibited the company from continuing the use of the land.  

Another project contractor, Shengping Precision Gas (Shanghai) Co., Ltd (SPG) also sued the company for the unpaid service for more than RMB 15 million. 

On September 2, 2020, the Wuhan Dongxihu District Court ruled a civil lawsuit between SPG and HSMC and frozen HSMC’s 15 million yuan property; 
Source: wenshu.court.gov.cn

Syd Lin, director at PricewaterhouseCoopers Hong Kong said that an incident like HSMC was not something beyond imagination, when the government promulgated tax/financial incentives, especially local subsidy, targeting special industry, it is always in the “news”.    
  
“I think one of the major reasons is that in the market, there exist too many “investors” making “opportunity shopping”, which means they are seeking for chances to get fund from the government (for various purposes), inflate the market value of the investment project and maybe sell the project to other parties with high premium.” said Mrs Lin.  

“They package themselves as “industry expertise” and leverage this “fame” or “respect”, probably, to make benefits…which is a real life case I handled a couple of years ago.” she added. 

Official documents from Dongxihu District of Wuhan state that the growth rate of fixed asset investment in the district had been a downward trend due to the slow progress of individual major projects. For instance, HSMC project had a total investment of 128 billion yuan, accounting for 44% of the total scale project in the region. The investment completion progress was only 11%, which is seriously lower than the progress of general investment projects in the district, according to the official document. 

The two government reports in 2019 mentioned that HSMC investment was seriously slower than expected. Souce: dxh.gov.cn

Where does the money go? 

The Wuhan Municipal Development and Reform Commission invested a total of RMB 15.3 billion to the HSMC to build a chip R&D and manufacturing base, using the world’s most advanced process technology to produce 10 nanometer chips, according to the official document which was later deleted. 

The 2019 Donghu District Government report showed that the total construction area of the HSMC project was 220,000 square meters, and it planned to purchase 1,200 sets of equipment. Souce: dxh.gov.cn

On December 22, 2019, HSMC purchased an brand new etching machine, extreme ultraviolet lithography (TWINSCAN NXT:1980Di - ASML) which will be used for chips production, and soon after it was mortgaged for RMB 581.8 million to the Market Supervision Administration according to the Qixin website. 

The ASML model TWINSCAN NXT: 1980Di etching machine, the official website does not show its single price. Source: asml.com

Combined with the announcements or indictments of the above three contractors, as of the third quarter of 2020, the total amount owed by HSMC to the contractors is approximately RMB 770 million.

Although the company encountered capital difficulties as claimed by the government to proceed the operation, its expenditure, the current cost was from the fund they received from the local government. 

Mrs Lin explains that the founders of Hongxin actually do not have any experience in the semiconductor sector and the business plan indicates that they do not need to inject cash to the project. All these facts show that it is likely to be a hoax, rather than a real investment with the purpose of making China great.

The company is 90% owned by a Beijing-based private firm and 10% by the government owned firm, according to Chinese corporate information platform Tianyancha. The Beijing-based firm is majority-owned by company Chairwoman Li Xueyan, who holds a 54% stake. Mo Sen, one of the company directors, holds the rest.

Before November 18, 2020, Beijing Guangliang Blueprint Technology Co., Ltd., which is owned by LI Xueyan, holds 90% of HSMC shares. Source: tianyancha.com

But the Beijing-based firm never put real money into the project according to local media, the cover.  

According to the HSMC 2019 Annual Report on the National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System, Beijing Guangliang Blueprint Technology Co., Ltd. paid RMB 1.8 billion and actually paid RMB 0. Source: National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System - WeChat Mini Program

Who is Wuhan Hongxing Semiconductor Manufacturing Company?

HSMC was founded in 2017, with the aim to build a 14 nanometer chip production line that can produce 30,000 wafers per month and a 7-nanometer chip production line of the same capacity. It is a joint venture between the Wuhan government and a Beijing-based firm called Guangliang Lantu Technology.

HSMC has proposed two phases of the projects. Phase one includes its main production facility and a research and development building which is 390,00 square meters, had partially completed, but needs more capital to move forward, according to the Dongxihu district government July’s report which has been removed online.  

The construction of its phase two facility has barely started and the company cannot apply for central government funding due to insufficient preparations.  

Mrs Lin said “Personally, I don’t think it is due to poor planning or funding shortage. It is just a hoax in the semiconductor economy booming days as per my understanding of various analyses on the public domain”.  

Shareholders

Li Xueyan has been in the market for many years. She has worked in ecological technology, sold shochu, set up restaurants, built gardens, and even sold Chinese medicine, but they are all small businesses with a maximum registered capital of only 4 million yuan.

On November 18, HSMC’s shareholder structure underwent a huge change. The previous board of directors Li Xueyan from Beijing Guangliang Blueprint Technology and others collectively stepped down, and the new shareholders were accepted by Wuhan New Technology Development Co. LTD, which was founded on November 6 and 100% owned by the Wuhan government, according to Tianyancha.

On November 18, 2020, HSMC was officially 100% owned by Wuhan New Technology Development Co. LTD. Source: Tianyancha.com

Li Xueyan, who was the former director of the firm, didn’t have experience in the semiconductor industry. She had been working in a small business, for example a restaurant and selling chinese medicine, which the register capital is RMB 4 million. 

Mrs Lin said “I am not familiar with their management team but open resources indicated that the purpose of the founders was not for a real development of semiconductor industry in China, but of course the experts they hired from Taiwan are good.” 

A Donghu government report published in 2019 states that there was insufficient audit of the process in the entry of enterprise. It also mentioned that there was no management review and the project did not conform with the plan.  

The report wrote that the government had insufficient audits in the process of auditing the entry of enterprises. Souce: dxh.gov.cn

Did the Wuhan local government hear the warning in advance?

On October 20, 2020, China's National Development and Reform Commission spokesperson Meng Wei, criticized some local governments "Some ‘three nos’ companies with no experience, no technology, and no talents have joined the integrated circuit industry. Some place did not have enough understanding of the law of integrated circuit development, and blindly launching projects, low-level repeated construction risks appear, and even some projects have stagnated and factory buildings are vacant, resulting in waste of resources."

The National Development and Reform Commission mentioned that some companies without any qualifications have participated in local chip investment projects. Souce: paper.people.com

He also emphasized that local governments should introduce supporting measures to standardize the market order for the development of the integrated circuit industry, and also establish a preventive mechanism, strengthen communication and coordination with banking institutions, investment funds to reduce investment in major integrated circuit projects risk.

The statement read “the National Development and Reform Commission will focus on guiding local governments to strengthen their understanding of the risks of major project construction, and in accordance with the principle of "who supports and is responsible", officials who cause major losses or trigger major risks will be notified and accountable.”

The central government will increase its efforts to hold officials responsible for the failure of integrated circuit investment projects. Souce: paper.people.com

It is unknown whether the purpose of Wuhan Xingong Technology Co., Ltd. and Wuhan Guangdan Blueprint Technology Co., Ltd., the shell companies owned by the Wuhan government, to take over HSMC and Beijing Guangdan Blueprint Technology Co., Ltd., respectively, is related to stricter regulatory policies. More importantly, LI Xueyan, the former principal of HSMC and Beijing Light Blueprint Company, has quietly separated from the relationship with these two companies and has left people’s sight.

What is the role of the government? 

Following months of delays in the construction of its US$20 billion manufacturing plant, Dongxihu district government in central province of Hubei took over the semiconductor factory, according to the latest corporate registration records compiled by Chinese data site Tianyancha.  

The firm is now under the control of the state assets supervision and administration commission for the Dongxihu district government in Wuhan. 

This is after the central government released a statement saying, a local government that did not take responsibility for a firm which is having a management problem, would face punishment. The local government later established a firm called Wuhan Guangliang Lantu Ltd in September 2020, took over the Beijing Guanglian Lantu Ltd, which shares 90% of HSMC. 

According to Article 20 of the Company Law of China, if a company shareholder abuses the independent status of the company as a legal person and the limited liability of shareholders, evades debts and seriously damages the interests of the company’s creditors, they shall bear joint liability for the company’s debts. Obviously, the original shareholders including Li Xuanyan do default on corporate tax evasion.

Specific content of Article 21 of the Company Law of the People's Republic of China. Souce: npc.gov.cn

In November, Beijing Guangliang Lantu Ltd ended their investment with HSMC, and Wuhan Xingong, the government owned firm, took over the share at HSMC. 

Since the takeover by the state own firm, Wuhan New Technology, the board members of HSMC were not questioned or investigated about the expense of the fund they received from the government. The taking over process was unclear as the local government took over HSMC through the newly established company.

Wuhan Xingong Technology Co., Ltd. paid HSMC's 2 billion planned capital. Source: qixin.com

The Chinese government is now prepared to invest more money, as much as RMB 9.5 trillion in technology in the 14th Five-Year Plan. This is set to be presented later this month at the Communist Party’s fifth Plenum. The subsidies will be offered to so-called ‘third generation’ semiconductor firms.
